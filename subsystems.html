AERODYNAMICS

The Aerodynamics subsystem ensures that the rocket follows the correct trajectory.


We design fins, nose cones, and perform CFD simulations to analyze how they affect the stability of the rocket.


Our major experimental projects include supersonic simulation, air brake design, and active control surfaces design.

AVIONICS

The Avionics subsystem develops the flight computer for our rocket.


It involves programming a multitude of sensors to track live rocket data. We also develop systems like air brakes and reaction control systems.


We also wish to explore relevant tasks, such as making ML algorithms, data compression, and video telemetry.

STRUCTURES

The Structures subsystem is responsible for the design of the rocket exterior and interior.


We perform composite and structural simulations of the rocket airframe and internal components. The team depends on us for the structural stability and safety of the rocket.

PAYLOAD AND RESEARCH

The Payload and Research subsystem develops the rocket payloads (The experiments conducted onboard during flight) and researches various aspects of rocketry and beyond. We also design payload deployment systems.


We currently put our research effort into rocket staging algorithms and supersonic aerodynamic design.

PROPULSION

The Propulsion subsystem develops the rocket engine.


We plan to build the first student-developed liquid rocket engine in India. We will hold a ground test of the largest student-built solid rocket motor in India soon.


Research into novel solid rocket fuels, motor ignitors, thrust vector control, and reaction control systems is underway.

MANAGEMENT

The Management subsystem is responsible for the smooth functioning of the team.


We monitor the team budget; plan and execute successful strategies for sponsorship. It is our responsibility to get the team all the resources it needs.


The team website and social media handles are under our care.
